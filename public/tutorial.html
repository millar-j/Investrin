What is a Stock?

A stock represents ownership in a company. When you buy a share, you own a piece of that company.

Why Do Companies Go Public?

To raise capital. Companies sell shares to investors in exchange for money to grow their business.

Risk vs Reward

Higher risk can mean higher reward, but it can also mean greater loss. Diversification helps reduce risk.

Dividends

Some companies share profits with investors in the form of dividends. These are like bonus payments.
